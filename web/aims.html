Aims

This course has several aims, and components corresponding to those aims:

  • Practice teaching physics.
  • Read key research literature on teaching and the classroom.
  • Experience and reflect on good teaching by others.
  • (Discuss responsible conduct of research.)
The last item is unlike the others; it is part of our grant administration requirements at NYU.

Expectations

Reasonable performance in this class is a pre-requisite to being assigned teaching positions within the Physics Department. Reasonable performance in the responsible conduct of research (RCR) part of this class is a pre-requisite to being supported on any external federal grant. I will not grade harshly, but I will expect the following of all students:

  • attendance: I will expect every student to come to every class. If you are going to miss a class for any reason, I expect a good excuse to be presented to me by email and in advance of the missed class. The only exceptions are medical excuses, which can be presented after, but once again I would like to get these by email.
  • written work: I will ask for written reactions to some of the papers we read, to the classroom visits we organize, and to some of the in-class presentations. These will not have to be long, but I expect them to be submitted by the given deadlines, and in (relatively) good English. If you have trouble with English, find resources to help you. Good English is important to teaching and doing research in physics.
